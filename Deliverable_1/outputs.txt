Shanly, an unincorporated locality and compact rural community situated within geographic Shawanaga Township, Parry Sound District, Ontario, Canada, resides on the northern shore of Parry Sound at the intersection of County Roads 28 and 29.
Established in 1836 by John Shanly and his brother-in-law Michael Condon, the settlement initially bore the name "Shanly's Corners" before being renamed in honor of its original founder, who migrated from County Galway, Ireland, in 1824.
The community experienced rapid growth, with a population reaching 350 by 1880.
In 1892, a post office was established, followed by the establishment of a local school in 1897.
Despite a decline in the town's fortunes following the closure of the local sawmill in the early 1970s, Shanly has undergone revitalization in recent years, buoyed by the emergence of new businesses and a burgeoning population.
Shanly now serves as a hub for optical science and technology research, thanks in part to its proximity to INO and other institutions in the field.

Carmine Gino Chiellino, born in 1946 in Pisa, Italy, is renowned as a composer, conductor, and pianist.

Pursuing further studies, he attended the University of Illinois, under the guidance of Otto Luening and Christoph von Dohnányi.
In 1978, Chiellino was awarded the prestigious Rome Prize, prompting his relocation to the United States to continue his studies at the University of California, Berkeley.
There, he completed his doctorate in 1984 under the supervision of Heinz Holliger.
Chiellino has held professorial roles in composition at the Conservatorio di Pisa since 1990 and at the University of Illinois since 2004.
Beyond academia, he has collaborated extensively with various ensembles, including the Chicago Symphony Orchestra.
